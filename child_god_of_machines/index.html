Translation Notes

  1. ↑ In Japan, the fiscal year begins in April.
  2. ↑ From what I can tell, a New Life Fair seems to be a sale in the spring, where second-hand or discounted goods — usually furniture or appliances — are sold for the new (fiscal) year. I'm guessing Makoto's talking about a seasonal Steam Sale-esque sale for video games here.
  3. ↑ Deus ex Machina (lit. god from the machine in Latin) is a plot device where an impossible situation suddenly gets solved by an unexpected event or force. The term comes from ancient Greek theater, when actors who played gods were lowered into the scenes with machines (imagine them being hoisted in the air by a crane).
  4. ↑ Chuunibyou (中二病, lit. middle school disease) is in essence, having a cringey middle school phase. Shinobu has a scout story named Chuunibyou.
  5. ↑ Shishou (師匠 lit. master) is an honorific for someone who has mastered a craft.
  6. ↑ This (機械仕掛けの神) is literally "god of machines".
