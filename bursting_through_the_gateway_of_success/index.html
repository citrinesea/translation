Translation Notes

  1. ↑ Makoto explains that the student president needs to say『参った』("I give up";"You got me"). In case you don't know what this means, to make someone "cry uncle" is to make them give in during a competition because they've been overpowered or strong-armed physically. I use it as it seems like a natural translation for the story, but keep in mind that what Makoto's saying probably includes non-physical competitions too.
  2. ↑ Hime (姫; princess) is part of Tori's last name Himemiya (姫宮), and 桃 (momo; peach) is part of his first name. With the peach (桃), Ibuki is referencing the story Momotaro (Peach Boy), where the hero Momotaro goes to Onigashima to fight a band of oni. With the princesses (姫), Ibuki may be referencing the oni Shuten-dōji, who according to legend, kidnapped and killed Kyoto noblewomen. Fun fact! This oni was said to live in Mt. Ibuki in some iterations of the legend.
  3. ↑ Oni are usually villains to defeat in folktales and legends; in Momotaro, Momotaro defeats a band of oni, and Shuten-dōji was slain by the samurai Minamoto Raikou.
