  2. ↑ AKATSUKI take a boat to get to the SS Preliminaries in Submarine because of Kuro's motion sickness.

  6. ↑ The name used here is マ爺ムン (majimun), a pun on マジムン (majimun, an evil spirit) and 爺 (jii, the uchinaaguchi word for grandpa or old man). Up to this point, Majimun has been referred to as 鬼 (Oni) and 沖縄の鬼 (Okinawa's Oni). You can read the Ibuki Roadmap for more details.
  7. ↑ As Keito explains, majimun are evil spirits in the Ryukyus. Shiisaa are said to protect people from majimun.
  8. ↑ Gajumaru are banyan trees native to the Ryukyus (now Okinawa) and tropical Asia. In Okinawan folklore, thought to be home to kijimuna, youkai similar to fairies. They look like young children, and are known to be mischevous. Among their folklore, they're good at fishing and often befriend humans, but get angered by those who are careless or destroy the environment.

  12. ↑ Hoshijima is a fictional island off the coast of mainland Okinawa; it's the home to a side branch of Shinkai religion worshippers. It was first mentioned in Stella Maris. You can read the Ibuki Roadmap for more details.

  17. ↑ Souma uses 貴様 (kisama) here, a rude, confrontational second-person pronoun. Keito usually uses it.
  18. ↑ 伝承 (denshou); Tradition in the sense of folklore, legends, passing things down.
  19. ↑ 伝統 (dentou); Tradition in the sense of convention.
  20. ↑ Because of its nuance and cultural significance for this story, I'll designate whenever 和 is used in the translation. 和 (wa) refers to a foundational cultural value in Japan that emphasizes social harmony through maintaining peaceful relationships within a group. 和 encourages compromise, consensus, and the avoidance of open conflict. Generally, it can refer to "Japanese-ness", and is used in words like 和風 (wafu, Japanese style).
  21. ↑ The harmony Keito says here is 調和 (chouwa), which is more of an objective harmony or reconciliation.
  22. ↑ 取り込む; to take something in, to bring in, to capture.
  23. ↑ 飲み込む; to take something into yourself, to swallow, to engulf.
  24. ↑ This is written out with the kanji 天翔華月.
  25. ↑ This is written out as 天翔KAGETSU.

  27. ↑ If an asterisk (*) follows after "harmony" for the rest of the passage, this is a mention of 和 (wa).
