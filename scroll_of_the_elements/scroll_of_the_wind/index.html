Translation Notes

  1. ↑ Souma refers to Kanata as 部長 (buchou, lit. chief ), or club president (Kanata's position in their club).
  2. ↑ Kanata's dialogue is completely in hiragana, likely to emphasize his slow, relaxed way of speaking. Some words are in brackets likely due to Japanese being difficult to read in full hiragana (it would be like reading English sentences without spaces between words).
  3. ↑ An influential leader from the 6th-7th centuries, Prince Shotoku is credited with modernizing government administration. He is also considered foundational for the spread of Buddhism in Japan. Read more here.
  4. ↑ From 緩い (loose, laid-back) + マスコットキャラクタ (mascot character), ゆるキャラ (yuruchara) are Japanese mascots. Yuru-chara are typically made to resemble and promote specific places, events or businesses. Their designs tend to be cute and sloppy. It's kind of Midori's whole life. Read more here.
  5. ↑ A famous Sengoku-era ninja. Hanzou served Tokugawa Ieyasu, one of Japan's three Great Unifiers. Read more about Hanzou here.
  6. ↑ In Japanese, words from other languages (such as English) are typically rendered in katakana. However, when Souma uses katakana words, they are all written in hiragana instead, likely to emphasize how he struggles with foreign words. These words are kept as written in hiragana, and put in "quotes".
  7. ↑ いい子 いい子 ("good kid, good kid") often includes head patting. There will be a lot of this.
  8. ↑ Tetora refers to Kuro as 大将 (taishou, lit. large general ), or boss. Not to be confused with Chiaki's 隊長 (taichou).
  9. ↑ This is likely about Kuro's work with Knights Killers, a temporary unit put together by Leo Tsukinaga. The story they appear in also happens during autumn.
