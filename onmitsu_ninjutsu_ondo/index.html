An Ondo is a distinct folk song genre defined by a swing beat in a 2/2 tempo. They're typically used during Obon celebrations as a community dance activity (bon odori). As part of the Ultimate 9 Songs Collection, the Ninja Association gets to sing an ondo song together! Listen to it here

The chants in this song are meant to be chanted by everyone. If you want to get in the spirit with Mayoi and Shinobu, use the chants in (bold paranthesis)!

Translation Notes

  1. ↑ A nine-character kuji, or hand-sign meditation, with a wide range of uses like praying for the elimation of disasters or victory in battle. Shinobu says it in Motor Show.
  2. ↑ Donden gaeshi (どんでん返し) is a revolving door used in fictional ninja hideouts - basically a trick wall that has secrets behind it. It more commonly refers to a literary plot twist, or the turn-table used on Kabuki stages to changes seasons, settings, or other scenes
  3. ↑ Fun fact: In Chuunibyou, Shinobu finds what seems to be a ninja scroll labeled "secret writings"
  4. ↑ 忍び (shinobi, meaning ninja) is where Shinobu's name comes from
  5. ↑ The word 迷い (mayoi, meaning hesitation) is a pun on Mayoi's name (マヨイ, written in katakana)
  6. ↑ The Five Release (or Escape) Jutsu are jutsu that ninjas use to hide or escape from enemies. Kanata and Chiaki perform the Water Escape Technique and the Blazing Escape Technique for the Scroll of the Elements live show.
