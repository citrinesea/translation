Translation Notes

  1. ↑ From the story Quarrel Festival. This will come up a lot.
  2. ↑ The Meiji Era (明治時代) (1868-1912) is the backdrop for Opera, marked by major political, economic and social change, rapid modernization, industrialization and Westernization in Japan.
  3. ↑ During the Meiji Era, houseboys were aspiring-students who lived in hosts' homes as they traveled to cities to study.
  4. ↑ Tsujigiri (辻斬り or 辻斬, lit. "crossroads killing") was a samurai-practice where samurai test a new sword's effectiveness by attacking (usually defenseless) passersby. In this story, they use Tsujigiri as a noun.
  5. ↑ Among other things, an affirmative and greeting Tetora uses
  6. ↑ Tetora refers to Kuro as 大将 (taishou, lit. high-ranking general ), or boss. Not to be confused with Chiaki's 隊長 (taichou).
  7. ↑ There's a similar line in Tough Guy about Kuro's forgetfulness. Cute!
  8. ↑ 道場破り (dojoyaburi, lit. dojo storming ). In martial arts, this is when someone visits rival martial arts schools and challenges its members to assert dominance over whose technique is better. If the dojo lost, its apprentices would abandon it for the winner's school. Read more here.
  9. ↑ We tried our best. He used 人徳 (lit. natural virtue) here. He's trying to get at the fact there's something fundamentally different about him and Kuro that's ultimately out of his control.
  10. ↑ Souma says the same thing in Scroll of the Elements. We love callbacks!
  11. ↑ Souma's running gag is regularly resorting to performing seppuku to make up for trouble. 切腹 (seppuku or harakiri, lit. belly-cutting ) was a form of ritualistic suicide samurai used for a variety of reasons. For Souma, that tends to be shame.
  12. ↑ This is actually an Ensemble Girls reference.
  13. ↑ "Hasumin fairies" on Google found this manga about a boy named Ian Hasumi who can see fairies. Do with that as you will.
  14. ↑ Kuro often refers to Keito as 旦那 (danna, lit. master), or "boss". Hasumi-no-danna is a variation of this.
  15. ↑ A reference to the Emperor's New Clothes.
  16. ↑ He references a Hannya (般若) mask, specifically. It's from Noh theatre.
  17. ↑ 幽霊部員 (yuurei buin, lit. ghost members). It's someone who's a member of a club on paper, but otherwise just doesn't show up or participate in club activities.
