Translation Notes

  1. ↑ The first four are likely a play on words for the idiom 花鳥風月 (kachōfūgetsu; lit. flower, bird, wind, moon). 花鳥風月 refers to the appreciation of nature and the natural world in Japanese culture. It also encourages mindfulness and inner peace.
  2. ↑ Darjeeling, from Darjeeling, India (renowned for its tea). Its taste profile is musky, spicy and floral, and is known as the "champagne of teas" for its light notes.
  3. ↑ Uva, from Sri Kanka. Its taste profile is earthy and bitter, with wintergreen essence.
  4. ↑ Java, from Indonesia. Its taste profile is malty, fruity and floral.
  5. ↑ Dooars, from the Dooars floodplains in India and Bhutan. Its taste profile is dark, bold, rich and malty.
  6. ↑ Blended tea is when different types of tea are mixed to create aromas and flavors that can't be reached with single teas.
